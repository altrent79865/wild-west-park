Boise’s Western-Themed Family Park

Round up the crew for rope bridges, splash zones, giant slides and events—free all day.

Why Families Love It

  • Always Free: Open daily, no admission.
  • Designed for All Ages: Toddler corral to teen challenge course.
  • Accessible: Ramps, adaptive swings.
  • Nature + Play: Native plants, pollinator paths, creek-side seating.
  • Community Hub: Storytimes, live music, seasonal festivals.

Explore the Park

Pioneer Town

A mini main street with climbable storefronts, a “bank” tunnel maze, swinging saloon doors, and a hand-crank bell tower for imaginative play.

Fort Williams

A towering fort with lookout towers, rope bridges, twisty slides, and a boulder scramble.

Silver Spur Splash Yard

Water jets, sluice channels, and pump-and-pour tables where kids “pan for gold.” Zero-depth entry and non-slip surfaces (Seasonal)

Rail Yard Run

A caboose climber, balance-beam “track,” spinner bowls, and a zip-line glide for big-kid thrills.

Little Homesteaders

A fenced toddler zone with low climbers, musical panels, gentle slides, and benches for relaxation.

Prairie Garden & Quiet Nooks

Butterfly-friendly plantings, shaded hammocks, porch-style swings, and calm corners for breaks.

Plan Your Visit

  • Location Just minutes from downtown Boise.
  • Hours Open daily, dawn to dusk (splash yard: late spring–early fall).
  • Cost Free admission.
  • Parking On-site lot + bike racks and ADA parking.
  • Restrooms Family restrooms with changing tables; water refill stations.
  • Food Picnics welcome; weekend food trucks seasonally.
  • Accessibility Smooth paths, ramped structures, adaptive swings, sensory maps.

Pro tip: Summer mornings are coolest—bring towels for the splash yard and sunscreen year-round.

Events & Programs

  • Music on the Meadow (summer): Local artists, blankets on the lawn.
  • Frontier Skills Day (seasonal): Knot-tying, orienteering, nature crafts.
  • Starlight Cinema (summer): Outdoor family movies at dusk.
  • Volunteer Saturdays: Garden tending and trail cleanups.

Safety & Park Etiquette

  • Trained park hosts on site during peak hours.
  • Shaded structures, temperature-tested surfaces, impact-safe materials.
  • Please supervise children, share kindly, and keep wheels slow in crowds.
  • Service animals welcome; pets leashed in designated areas only.

FAQs

Is it really free?

Yes—admission and most programs are free. Some special events may have optional concessions.

Are there age limits?

Zones are marked by recommended ages (2–5, 5–12, 10+), but families can explore together.

What about weather closures?

We’re open in most conditions barring inclement weather. The splash yard and some structures are open seasonally.
